**Date:** 24 April 2026  
**Author:** John Bowman  
**Article Type:** News/Personal Narrative  
**Status:** Design approved, ready for implementation

## Overview

A humorous, chronological narrative about the author's 2024–2026 experiment with AI music generation using Suno and SoundON Global distribution. The article documents an unexpected outcome: 88 million total views across ~60 tracks, with 9 tracks exceeding 1 million views each—culminating in the reveal that the author earned approximately £600 from the entire project. The piece is aimed at people considering trying AI music generation and emphasizes that while monetization is negligible, the creative experiment itself is worthwhile.

## Content Structure

### 1. Introduction (2–3 paragraphs, ~150 words)

**Purpose:** Hook the reader and establish the premise.

**Approach:**
- Open with the experiment setup: "In 2024, I decided to try AI music generation using Suno. It seemed like a silly idea at the time..."
- Briefly acknowledge the outcome was unexpected
- Signal the tone: self-aware, somewhat tongue-in-cheek
- End with a question that makes them want to read on: "What actually happened was both surprising and a cautionary tale."

**Tone:** Casual, self-deprecating, curious

---

### 2. The Setup (1–2 paragraphs, ~200 words)

**Purpose:** Explain what Suno is, why the author tried it, and set expectations.

**Content:**
- What is Suno? (AI music generation tool, brief overview of how it works)
- Why did you try it? (What prompted the experiment in 2024?)
- What kind of music did you make? (Vague reference to the tracks being "embarrassing" — save details for later)
- What were you expecting? (Honest about initial expectations being low)

**Tone:** Practical but self-aware. Not a technical breakdown, more of a "here's what I was thinking" narrative.

---

### 3. The Distribution (1–2 paragraphs, ~200 words)

**Purpose:** Explain SoundON Global and why you chose it; set up the surprise.

**Content:**
- What is SoundON Global? (Distribution platform for independent artists)
- Why upload Suno tracks there? (Curiosity, why not?)
- What were you expecting to happen? (Modest reach, some listens maybe)
- Signal that reality diverged from expectations

**Tone:** Honest about low expectations; narrative turning point

---

### 4. The Long Game (2–3 paragraphs, ~300 words)

**Purpose:** Build the story arc of watching views accumulate over time.

**Content:**
- Timeline: 2024 → 2026. This wasn't a quick spike; views built up gradually over months
- Early months: checking the numbers occasionally, modest growth
- Mid-period: realizing the tracks are actually getting traction
- Progression: watching the view count climb, some tracks breaking into 6 figures, then 7 figures
- Emotional arc: confusion → surprise → genuine amazement
- Narrative beat: "Then 9 of them crossed 1 million views. Each."

**Tone:** Genuine surprise and progression. This is the setup for the punchline.

---

### 5. The Numbers (1 paragraph, ~150 words)

**Purpose:** Present the full scope of the reach.

**Content:**
- Total: 88 million views across ~60 tracks
- Range: from a few thousand to over 1 million
- The standout: 9 tracks with 1M+ views
- Put it in perspective: "Equivalent to [comparison]" (optional, if helpful)
- Signal the twist: "Which sounded incredible until I looked at the earnings."

**Tone:** Clear, factual, with a slight build toward the reveal

---

### 6. The Punchline (1–2 paragraphs, ~200 words)

**Purpose:** The reveal—and the humor.

**Content:**
- "I made £600."
- The math that makes it absurd: 88M views ÷ £600 = the cost per million views
- Breakdown: what the payout structure actually looks like (streaming rates, platform cuts, etc.)
- The gap: "88 million people heard my music. I could afford a decent dinner."
- Use short, punchy sentences here
- The humor: not bitter, just the sheer absurdity of the mismatch

**Tone:** Dry humor, self-aware about the ridiculousness. Not complaining, just stating facts.

---

### 7. Why You Won't Share Them (1 paragraph, ~100 words)

**Purpose:** Address the obvious question: "Can I listen?"

**Content:**
- Two reasons: (A) the tracks are genuinely embarrassing, (B) legal uncertainty around AI-generated music makes you cautious about promotion
- Not defensive—just honest
- Brief, matter-of-fact

**Tone:** Candid, slightly self-conscious but not apologetic

---

### 8. The Takeaway (2 paragraphs, ~250 words)

**Purpose:** Close with a balanced perspective for the target audience.

**Content:**
- Reframe the experience: it wasn't about money, it was about the creative tool itself
- The genuine value: Suno is accessible, it's fun, you can generate music quickly
- Realistic perspective: if you're thinking about trying AI music generation, do it—but understand the monetization math is not the draw
- What might make it worthwhile for others: (portfolio building, creative exploration, learning the tool, etc.)
- Closing statement: Something like "Go in with eyes open, but it's worth the experiment"

**Tone:** Balanced, pragmatic, encouraging without overselling

**Target Word Count:** 1,200–1,500 words  
**Rationale:** Substantial enough to tell the full story without filler. Mid-range for a narrative article.

## Tone & Voice

- **Primary tone:** Humorous, self-deprecating, surprised
- **Sentence structure:** Varied—short, punchy sentences on the £600 reveal; longer, explanatory sentences elsewhere
- **Contractions:** Yes ("don't," "it's," "I'd," etc.)
- **Perspective:** First-person narrative
- **Stance:** Honest and candid; one stated opinion that could be debated (e.g., whether the time investment was worth it)
- **No banned words:** No "delve," "leverage," "unlock," "seamlessly," "empower," or similar AI-speak
- **Active voice:** "I made £600" not "£600 was earned"
