The Gen Z Translator converts standard English into Gen Z slang - or decodes Gen Z text back into plain English. Paste any sentence, paragraph or document and hit Translate. You can also swap the direction using the arrow button, so it works both ways. It is built for marketers, teachers, curious parents, or anyone who wants a laugh.

The tool runs on Claude Haiku by Anthropic. Your input is sent securely to Anthropic's API and is not stored or used to train AI models. The tool complies with UK GDPR and the Anthropic Privacy Policy as of March 2026. Results are intended for entertainment - not all slang will be current or appropriate for every context.

Frequently Asked Questions
Which AI model powers this tool?
The tool uses Claude Haiku by Anthropic - specifically the claude-haiku-4-5 model. It is a fast, lightweight model that is well-suited to short-form style transformation tasks like this one.
How accurate is the Gen Z slang?
Claude is trained on large amounts of real online text, so results are generally natural and contextually appropriate. That said, slang evolves quickly and regional variations exist. Some outputs may feel slightly dated or miss nuance. The tool is a starting point, not a guarantee of authenticity.
Can I use this for marketing content?
You can use it to generate ideas or check tone, but always have someone from your target audience review the output before publishing. Misused slang can land badly. The tool is most useful as a creative prompt or research aid rather than a production pipeline.
Is my text stored or used to train the AI?
No. Your input is sent to Anthropic's API solely to process the translation request and is not retained or used for training. See the Anthropic Privacy Policy for full details. JohnB.io does not log or store the content you submit.
Why is there a disclaimer about offensive content?
Gen Z slang includes terms that can carry different meanings depending on context, community or tone. The tool is designed to be fun and educational, not offensive. If a result feels inappropriate for your audience, discard it - the AI is not always aware of every cultural nuance.
How It Works
  1. Type or paste your text into the left panel. You can also upload a .txt, .doc or .docx file using the Choose File button.
  2. Check the translation direction. By default the tool translates from English to Gen Z. Click the swap button between the two panels to reverse the direction - useful for decoding Gen Z text into standard English.
  3. Click Translate. Your text is sent to Claude Haiku, which rewrites it in the target style using current Gen Z vocabulary and phrasing patterns.
  4. Read the result in the right panel. The output preserves the meaning of your original text while changing the register and vocabulary.
  5. Copy or share the result directly from the output panel, or run it through again with the direction swapped to check the translation back.
Key Points
  • Two-way translation. Translate English into Gen Z slang or convert Gen Z text back into standard English - the swap button switches direction instantly.
  • Useful for marketers and educators. Brands targeting younger audiences use it to sanity-check campaign copy. Teachers use it to decode messages or create relatable examples for students.
  • AI-driven, not dictionary-driven. Because the tool uses a language model rather than a fixed word-swap list, the output reads naturally in context rather than producing awkward literal substitutions.
  • Built for fun, not formal use. The disclaimer is on the page for a reason - results can be unpredictable and slang evolves quickly. Always review the output before using it professionally.
  • No account needed. Free to use with a daily per-IP limit to keep it available for everyone. Resets at midnight UTC.
